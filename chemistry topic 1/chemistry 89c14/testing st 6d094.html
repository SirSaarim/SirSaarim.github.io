  • describe formation of nitrogen oxides - NOx
    1. heat is released during the combustion of fuel (or just in high temperatures), giving oxygen and nitrogen the energy to react
    1. nitric oxide is further oxidised during combustion to form nitrogen dioxide
  • describe the formation of ozone
    1. nitrogen dioxide (NO2) undergoes photodissociation in the troposphere in the presence of high energy visible light and ultraviolet radiation (hf) from the sun (sunlight)
    1. a highly reactive oxygen atom (called oxygen radical) is formed in the reaction. the oxygen radical further reacts with oxygen gas in the troposphere forming ozone (O3)
  • NOx - visual pollutant

    produces the brown haze which surrounds cities experiencing photochemical smog

  • O3 - respiratory irritant
    • damages tissue lining of lungs
    • elderly, young and those prone to asthma and bronchitis are more susceptible
  • O3 - impacts plant growth
    • limits gas exchange in leaves
    • during photosynthesis, ozone & CO2 enter stoma on leaves → stomata end up closing
    • it is a strong oxidant therefore affects photosynthesis and other cellular reactions
    • increases their susceptibility to disease and extreme weather conditions such as droughts
  • O3 - polymers
    • ozone can damage elastomers (elastic polymers) → makes them more brittle and susceptible to cracking under stress
    • causes degradation of polymer chains by oxidising and breaking carbon-carbon double bonds C=C
    • less double carbon bonds → reduced elasticity
    • ozone leaves the polymer chains where C=C bonds exist, producing smaller molecules and thus reducing the strength of interactions due to dispersion forces
    • as a strong oxidant, ozone also causes changes in the chemical composition of the polymers, resulting in weaker interactions between polymer chains

    summary

    • makes them more brittle
    • reduces their elasticity
    • damages them by breaking C=C bonds
    • reduces strength of interactions between polymer chains
  • what is a catalytic converter?
    • a catalytic converter is a device that is fitted to the exhaust system of a vehicle with the aim of reducing emissions of nitrogen oxides and other primary pollutants from motor vehicles
    • introduced in vehicles in efforts of reducing the production of photochemical smog
    • the purpose is to remove CO, NOx and unburnt hydrocarbons through the use of platinum group metals as a catalyst
    • made up of open porous matrix coated with the platinum group metals
    • precursors of photochemical smog - ozone, NOx
  • what three pollutants does the three-way catalytic converter process?
    • carbon monoxide
    • nitrogen oxide
    • unburnt hydrocarbons
      • carbon monoxide + unburnt hydrocarbons → products of incomplete combustion

  • describe properties of the troposphere
    • amongst all atmospheric layers, the troposphere contains 80% of all gases
    • it is the lowest layer - closest to the earth's surface
    • oxygen and nitrogen make up 99% of the gases within this specific layer
  • what is photochemical smog?
    • photochemical smog is a haze comprised of a mixture of chemicals and airborne pollutants which react with sunlight to produce air pollution in the troposphere
    • smog → smoky fog
  • 4 examples of secondary pollutants
    • ozone - O3
    • sulfur trioxide - SO3
    • sulphuric acid - H2S04
    • nitric acid - HNO3
  • what is temperature inversion?
    • normally, low troposphere has warmer air, this heat naturally rises above where the air is much cooler
    • temperature inversion exists when there is warm layer of air which acts a blanket, trapping the cool air below
      • this can be caused by peak traffic hours when a lot of photochemical smog is produced which is then trapped in the lower troposphere due to temperature inversion
  • NOx - eye irritant

    react with water / moisture to produce acids that irritate eyes

  • how do catalytic converters reduce the harm of primary pollutants?
    • use a metal catalyst
    • inside a 3-way catalytic converter, a ceramic honeycomb structure is coated with finely divided platinum, palladium and rhodium. the honeycomb structure increases the surface area of the catalyst which subsequently increases the rate of conversion of primary pollutants into less harmful substances
